# COMMST 204 — Key terms (Week 7)

Note: this file was `key.txt` in `quiz2/`; content is communications / org behaviour, not Music 140.

Organizational culture (Schein): shared basic assumptions a group develops while handling external adaptation and internal integration.

Visible in: actions, stories, artifacts. Also: values and assumptions.

Leaders shape: values, attitudes, ways of doing things. They still negotiate cultural realities.

Culture may be deliberately created or emerge organically.

Groups/teams: core to how people experience organizations. Formal groups have explicit rules and leadership; informal ones develop norms and emergent leaders.

Group advantages: varied expertise, diversity, discussion. Disadvantages: inefficiency, communication load, coasting, need for care and reflection.

Learning group (Adler): purpose is to increase each member’s knowledge and skill.

Self-concept (Beebe): your own picture of who you are; not the same as others’ perceptions. Decenter / perspective-taking matters.

Self-esteem low: sensitive to slights, harder to contribute. High: thicker skin, may resist others’ views.

Perception: confirmation bias, self-serving bias, attribution issues, negotiation of meaning, factions.

Task roles (Fujishin): move discussion toward the task. Maintenance roles: support group climate. Self-centered roles (Eisenberg): dominate, divert, inappropriate.

Democracy in small groups vs autocracy: equality vs hierarchy, persuasion vs top-down rules, consensus/vote vs leader-only decisions, multidirectional vs downward communication.

Demos (Gastil): people governing themselves democratically.
