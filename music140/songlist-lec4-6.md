### Reference: Names to Know

**Performers / Artists**
- **Little Richard (Richard Penniman)** – "Tutti Frutti"; early rock and roll; left music 1957 to become a priest
- **Pat Boone** – cover artist; "Tutti Frutti" cover; smoother white pop versions of R&B songs
- **The Chords** – "Sh-Boom" original (Cat Records)
- **The Crew Cuts** – "Sh-Boom" cover; pop/mainstream version
- **Bill Haley (born 1925)** – Bill Haley and the Comets; "Rock Around the Clock"; too old for the teen audience
- **Elvis Presley (1935–1977)** – "That's Alright Mama," "Blue Moon of Kentucky," "Heartbreak Hotel," "Hound Dog," etc.; Sun Records → RCA; Rockabilly; died Aug 16, 1977 (drug OD, age 42)
- **Chuck Berry (born Oct 18, 1926)** – "Maybellene," "Johnny B. Goode"; Chess Records; always played guitar; wrote own songs; self-conscious businessman
- **Jerry Lee Lewis** – "Great Balls of Fire"; piano; career ended May 1958 (married 13-year-old cousin); "rough diamond"
- **Buddy Holly** – "Peggy Sue" (1956); died Feb 1959 in plane crash with Ritchie Valens and The Big Bopper
- **Ritchie Valens** – died in Buddy Holly's plane crash, Feb 1959
- **The Big Bopper (JP Richardson)** – died in Buddy Holly's plane crash, Feb 1959
- **Chubby Checker** – "The Twist"; Dance Craze era
- **Little Eva (Eva Boyd)** – "The Locomotion"; Carole King's babysitter
- **Bobby Vinton** – "Blue Velvet" (1963); Teen Idol
- **The Ronettes** – "Be My Baby" (1963); girl group; Phil Spector production
- **Les Paul (1915–2009)** – "Sitting on Top of the World"; invented solid-body electric guitar ("The Log" / Gibson Les Paul); pioneered multitrack recording
- **Mary Ford** – vocal partner of Les Paul on recordings

**Industry / Behind-the-Scenes Figures**
- **Alan Freed** – DJ credited with naming "Rock and Roll"; charged in Payola scandal; career destroyed
- **Sam Phillips** – owner of Sun Records (Memphis); signed Elvis, BB King, Johnny Cash; sold Elvis's contract to RCA
- **Colonel Tom Parker** – Elvis's manager; arranged TV appearances; steered Elvis toward pop; bought Elvis's contract for $35,000
- **Bob Wills** – "Ida Red" (the song Chuck Berry rewrote into "Maybellene")
- **Don Kirshner / Al Nevins** – ran Aldon Music (Brill Building)
- **Gerry Goffin / Carole King** – Brill Building songwriting team; wrote "The Locomotion"
- **Neil Diamond** – Brill Building songwriter (wrote "Sweet Caroline")
- **Jack Mullin** – US Marine engineer who discovered the Magnetophone; became Bing Crosby's audio assistant
- **Bing Crosby** – major star of 1930s–50s; used Magnetophone to pre-record broadcasts; funded AMPEX; gave Les Paul one of the first reel-to-reel recorders
- **Phil Spector** – producer; "Wall of Sound" technique; "Be My Baby" (The Ronettes, 1963)
- **Hank Ballard** – wrote "The Twist" (Chubby Checker's hit)

**Labels**
- **Sun Records** – Sam Phillips's Memphis independent; early home of Elvis, BB King, Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis
- **Chess Records** – Chicago independent; Chuck Berry, Muddy Waters; told artists to write their own songs
- **Specialty Records** – Little Richard's label
- **Cat Records** – The Chords' label (original "Sh-Boom")
- **RCA** – major label; Colonel Tom Parker brought Elvis here; national distribution power
