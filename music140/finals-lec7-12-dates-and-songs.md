## Songs (why they matter)

Surf: *Misirlou*, *Wipe Out* — instrumentals. *Surfin’ U.S.A.* — Chuck Berry / *Sweet Little Sixteen*; stop-time; BG vocals. *Good Vibrations* — cello, theremin, key changes, multitrack.

Beatles: *Rock Island Line* (skiffle). *Love Me Do*. *Please Please Me*. *She Loves You* (first big US hit). *Yesterday* — strings, serious lyrics, still AABA. *Tomorrow Never Knows* — drone/tamboura, Leslie, loops, backwards; hard to play live. *Strawberry Fields*. *A Day in the Life* — stitched sections, aleatory orchestra; concept-album idea.

Stones: *I Wanna Be Your Man*. *King Bee* (Slim Harpo). *Satisfaction* — riff, attitude.

Soul: *You’d Better Shop Around* — Motown polish, choreography. *Stop in the Name of Love* — HDH, vibraphone. *Green Onions* — Stax, integrated band. *Try a Little Tenderness* — Stax energy. *Soul Man* — groove. *Respect* — Otis tune flipped; anthem.

Funk: JB early; *Live at Apollo*; *Cold Sweat*; *Sex Machine* — rhythm/riffs, two chords, cyclical.

Folk / counterculture: *This Land…*; *Tom Dooley*; Dylan *Hard Rain*, *Like a Rolling Stone* (electric 1965). *Truckin’*. *White Rabbit*. *Voodoo Child*.

Events: Woodstock; Altamont; *Ohio* (Kent State).

Metal / hard rock: *War Pigs*; Deep Purple *Concerto* / *Highway Star*; Zep *Whole Lotta Love*; AC/DC *Highway to Hell*; Metallica *Master of Puppets*.

Hip hop: *Signifyin’ Monkey*; *Rapper’s Delight* (Chic groove); *Walk This Way*.

## Hippie aesthetic (Rock ’n’ Roll → Rock)

Singles → albums. Dancing → listening. Entertainers → artists. *Sgt. Pepper* as album/studio pivot.

## Beatles vs Stones (class)

Beatles: often working-class roots, clean middle-class image. Stones: often middle-class roots, “dangerous” working-class image.
