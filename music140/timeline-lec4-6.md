# Timeline of Events — Lectures 4–6

---

## Pre-1950s

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1920 | Payola (bribes in the music industry) already in practice |
| Late 1920s | Blind Lemon Jefferson records early version of "That's Alright Mama" |
| 1938 | "Ida Red" — Bob Wills and the Texas Playboys (later rewritten by Chuck Berry) |
| 1946 | "Blue Moon of Kentucky" recorded — Bill Monroe |
| 1948 | Bing Crosby gives one of the first reel-to-reel tape recorders to Les Paul |
| 1950 | "Blue Velvet" composed |
| 1951 | "Blue Velvet" becomes a hit for Tony Bennett (TPA era) |

---

## 1952–1953

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1952 | Bill Haley and the Saddlemen rename themselves **Bill Haley and the Comets** |
| 1953 | Elvis Presley (age 18) meets Sam Phillips and signs with Sun Records |
| 1953 | **"Sitting On Top of the World"** — Les Paul with Mary Ford; early showcase of multitrack recording |

---

## 1954

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| May 1954 | **Brown v. Board of Education** — Supreme Court rules segregated schools unconstitutional |
| June 1954 | **"Sh-Boom"** — The Chords (Cat Records); #3 R&B, #5 Pop |
| June 1954 | **"Sh-Boom" cover** — The Crew Cuts; becomes #1 Pop for 9 weeks |
| July 1954 | **"That's Alright Mama"** — Elvis Presley (Arthur Crudup); played 13–17 times in two hours on Dewey Phillips' radio show |
| 1954 | **"Blue Moon of Kentucky"** — Elvis Presley's country/pop reworking of the Bill Monroe song |
| 1954 | **"Rock Around the Clock"** — Bill Haley and the Comets recorded (Freedman/Myers) |

---

## 1955

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1955 | **"Rock Around the Clock"** rises to popularity; featured in the film *Blackboard Jungle* |
| 1955 | **"Tutti Frutti"** — Little Richard (Penniman, LaBostrie); #2 R&B, #17 Pop; released on Specialty Records |
| July 1955 | **Chuck Berry signs with Chess Records** |
| 1955 | **"Maybellene"** — Chuck Berry (rewritten from "Ida Red"); #1 R&B, #5 Pop |
| December 1955 | **Rosa Parks** refuses to give up her bus seat — triggers the **Montgomery Bus Boycott** |

---

## 1956

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1956 | **"Tutti Frutti" cover** — Pat Boone; #10 Pop (no R&B chart) |
| 1956 | Race-based cover versions stop being profitable |
| 1956 | **Rock 'n' Roll** term widely attributed to DJ **Alan Freed** (ABC) |
| 1956 | Colonel Tom Parker signs Elvis to **RCA**; puts him on national TV **11 times** |
| April–May 1956 | **"Heartbreak Hotel"** — Elvis Presley; #1 Pop, #5 R&B |
| June 1956 | Elvis performs "Hound Dog" on **Texaco Star Theatre** — generates 10,000 letters of outrage |
| ~July 1956 | Elvis performs "Hound Dog" on **The Steve Allen Show** (toned-down, humiliating performance) |
| Aug–Dec 1956 | **"Hound Dog," "Don't Be Cruel," "Love Me Tender"** — all chart #1 on Pop, Country & R&B simultaneously |
| 1956 | **"Peggy Sue"** — Buddy Holly |

---

## 1957

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1957 | **The Little Rock Nine** — first Black students to integrate a white school; National Guard called as escort |
| After 1957 | Elvis stops charting on R&B; Parker steers career toward mainstream Pop |
| October 1957 | **Little Richard** quits rock and roll after a plane engine explodes mid-flight; becomes a minister |

---

## 1958

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1958 | **"Johnny B. Goode"** — Chuck Berry; #2 R&B, #8 Pop |
| 1958 | **"Great Balls of Fire"** — Jerry Lee Lewis |
| March 1958 | **Elvis drafted into the U.S. Army**; Colonel Tom pre-records many singles for release during absence |
| May 1958 | **Jerry Lee Lewis** scandal: marriage to his 13-year-old cousin becomes public; dropped by Sun Records |

---

## 1959

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| February 1959 | **Buddy Holly** dies in a plane crash along with **Ritchie Valens** and **The Big Bopper (J.P. Richardson)** — later called "The Day the Music Died" |
| December 1959 | **Chuck Berry arrested** under the Mann Act (transporting a woman across state lines for illegal purposes) |
| 1959 | **End of the Golden Age of Rock and Roll** (1954–1959) |

---

## 1960–1963 (The In-Between Years)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| March 1960 | **Elvis returns from the Army** |
| March 1961 | Elvis's last live performance for **7 years**; transitions to making movies full-time |
| 1962 | **"The Locomotion"** — Little Eva (written by Goffin & Carole King); part of the Dance Craze era |
| 1963 | **"Blue Velvet"** — Bobby Vinton; brings back TPA-style sound for teen market |
| 1963 | **"Be My Baby"** — The Ronettes (Barry, Greenwich, Spector); showcases Phil Spector's "Wall of Sound" |

---

## 1964 & Beyond

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| February 1964 | **The Beatles arrive in the US** — end of the In-Between Years |
| 1968 | **Elvis's "Comeback Special"** — first song performed: "That's Alright Mama" |
| 1970 | **Elvis's Las Vegas Residency** begins; physical and drug-related health declines |
| August 16, 1977 | **Elvis Presley found dead** from drug overdose — age 42 |
