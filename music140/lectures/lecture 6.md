# Technology and Rise of the Producer
- Fundamental shift in how popular music is recorded 
- Jack Mullin
	- Sergeant in US Marine Corps of Engineers 
	- Mentioned Hitler - Nazi Germany invented rockets (V2s)
	- On the way to England, they are the first objects that went into space
	- Verner Von Brown - a rocket scientist in Nazi Germany
		- Wanted to go to America 
		- Thanks to these people USA won the space race
		- Jack Mullin discovered the **Magnetophone**
		- Nazi germany created first physical tape to tape recording
	- With the Magnetophone, he records a string classical recording and shows an audience that the Magnetophone plays music amazing
- Bing Crosby
	- Major star of the major 30s, 40s and 50s
	- Major film star as well
	- Had a weekly radio show (live)
	- The magnetophone was so good that he wanted to use it to pre-record a weekly broadcast
	- You can edit and manipulate the information
		- With the tape, you can cut off pieces of recordings that you don't want, so your recordings sound much nicer compared to live shows 
		- Jack Mullin becomes Bing Crosby's assistant 
	- Creates AMPEX - one of the biggest tape manufacturers of the world
	- 1948, Bing Crosby give one of the first commercially produced reel-to-reel tape  recorders to Les Paul

- Les Paul (1915-2009)
	- Super obsessed about technology and recording music
	- Popular guitarist
	
	- In an acoustic guitar, even a good one, the sound dies away quickly
	- Les builds a guitar out of solid wood - makes the most amazing sound
		- Called "the Log"
		- This is known as the "Solid Body" electric guitar
		- The Gibson "Les Paul"
			- Incredibly heavy instrument
	- He also is fascinated by recordings
		- his goal is to create recordings by layering performances
		- He uses the Magnetophone, and has the idea to split the tape into two sections and record two different instruments (layer)
		- You can record multiple independent streams of audio AKA multitrack recording
		- He changes the equalization, tone, volume and come back years later and change stuff
		- In 4-8 tracks in Les Paul's time
		- In the 1970s, the records can go up to 16-tracks

	- **Sitting On Top of the World Les Paul with Mary Ford, 1953**
		- Les Paul does all the instruments
		- Mary Ford does all the vocals
		- uses multitrack recording
		- Multitrack recording redefines the process of recording. 
		
		*Reproduction (taking a moment in time) to Production (creating moments by putting pieces together.)*
		
		- Discovered "Varied Speed Recordings"
			- Using technology to make it sound faster to make sounds that human's can't make 
		- This performance occurred by recording each layer over the period of several days
