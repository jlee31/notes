- Early 17th century, John Napier invented logarithms
Around 1640, Blaise Pascal, a leading French mathematician, constructed a mechanical adding device based on a design described by Greek mathematician Hero of Alexandria
- Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz developed logic in a formal way in 1702 deveoping binary.
- Nit's saying: If you said 1+1=0 is insane you never thought of the idea of binary or something
- In 1961, Norbert Wiener suggested that Leibniz should be considered the patron saint of cybernetics. (Cybernetics : study of circular processes with feedback loops and recursion.)
- From the industrial revolution, Joseph Marie Jacquard's loom in 1801 used punch cards where the punched holes indicates 1 and non-punched indicate 0. While it wasn't a computer, it showed that machines made from binary is possible.
